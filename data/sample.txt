Vector databases store high-dimensional numeric representations of data called embeddings.
An embedding maps text, images, or other inputs into a fixed-length vector space.
Similar meanings tend to produce vectors that point in similar directions in that space.

Cosine similarity measures the angle between two vectors, ignoring their magnitude to some extent.
It is computed as the dot product divided by the product of the vector lengths.
Search systems embed the query and compare it to stored vectors to find nearest neighbors.
